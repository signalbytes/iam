3 Smart Home Gadgets Under $30 That Automated My Morning

I used to hate mornings. Stumbling around in the dark, waiting for the coffee machine to heat up, and forgetting to turn on the porch light. It was chaotic.

Then I spent less than $30 on a few strategic smart home gadgets. I didn't rewire my house or buy a $3,000 smart fridge. I just bought smart plugs and set up voice routines.

The Power of the Smart Plug

Smart plugs are the unsung heroes of home automation. You plug them into a standard outlet, and then plug your "dumb" appliance into them. Suddenly, your $20 coffee maker is connected to the internet.

I use three specific plugs to run my morning:

  • The Coffee Plug: Turns on at 6:15 AM so the water is hot when I walk into the kitchen.
  • The Lamp Plug: Fades on gradually starting at 6:00 AM to simulate a sunrise.
  • The Heater Plug: Warms up the bathroom for 15 minutes before my shower.

Tying it Together with Voice Routines

Hardware is useless without software. I use Google Assistant (though Alexa works just as well) to tie these plugs together into a single "Good Morning" routine.

When my alarm goes off, the routine triggers. I don't have to speak to a speaker; the alarm dismissal is the trigger.

The Morning Automation Checklist

Ready to build your own automated morning? Follow this exact checklist to get started.

[ ] Buy a 3-pack of Wi-Fi Smart Plugs (e.g., Kasa or Wyze) - ~$25.
[ ] Plug in your coffee maker, bedside lamp, and bathroom heater.
[ ] Leave the physical switches on those devices in the "ON" position.
[ ] Open your Google Home or Alexa app.
[ ] Create a new Routine triggered by "Alarm Dismissed" or a specific time.
[ ] Add actions: Turn on Coffee Plug, Turn on Heater Plug, Fade on Lamp Plug.
