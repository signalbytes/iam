Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity basics that protect your data

Practical steps to secure accounts, devices, backups, privacy, and everyday browsing habits without turning security into a full-time job.

Core checklist

Your baseline security setup

These are the habits that remove the most common weaknesses first. For most people, they matter far more than buying another tool.

  • Use a password manager and avoid reusing passwords across sites.
  • Turn on multi-factor authentication for email, banking, storage, and social accounts.
  • Keep phones, laptops, browsers, and apps updated.
  • Review recovery email addresses and phone numbers on major accounts.
  • Back up important files to both cloud storage and one offline copy.
  • Pause before clicking links in urgent-looking emails or messages.
  • Lock devices with a real passcode, not just swipe access.

Where people usually get caught

Most compromise starts with a weak reused password, a fake login page, an old unpatched device, or a missing backup. Fix those first.

Accounts & identity

Secure the accounts that can reset everything else

Your email account is the real control tower. If that is taken over, many other services can be reset from there. Give email, password storage, and banking accounts the strongest protection first.

Quick weekly check

  • Check for failed sign-in alerts.
  • Review active sessions on major accounts.
  • Delete old apps or browser extensions you do not use.
  • Make sure backups are still running.
